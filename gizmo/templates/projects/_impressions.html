Impressions from a Lost World

2017, Pocumtuck Valley Memorial Association

About this site

This website explores the mid-19th century discovery of strange, 3-toed footprints, later understood as dinosaur tracks, in the Connecticut River Valley in Massachusetts and Connecticut. The significance of this first scientific discovery extended far beyond the emerging scientific community, with implications that had a profound effect not only on science but also on American religion, arts, and culture.

Story Introduction

The primary content of this site is told in six stories. Each story begins with an introductory page that was lavishly illustrated by local artist Monica Vachula. Monica did exhaustive research to ensure that her paintings were historically accurate.

Interactive Map

The protagonist of this website is Edward Hitchcock who, among many other professions, was a cartographer. He created the first geographic map of Massachusetts. The interactive map lets viewers overlay various historic maps onto present-day roads and terrain and then plot sites on it.

Find the Footprints

This activity challenges the user to closely observe the fossil trackways and identify the dinosaur footprints. It's harder than it sounds!
